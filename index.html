Bio

About Me

I'm an aspiring researcher with a background in physics and environmental science. I have a passion for fluid dynamics, sensor development, and large data sets.

I was born outside of Princeton, New Jersey and grew up in a town that boasted more cows than people. When I was little I dreamed of being an engineer due to a fascination with Legos and bridges. However, a high school physics class made me see the beauty of physics.

At Dickinson I studied applied physics working it into every aspects of my classes. Some of my favorite courses were Oceanography and Environmental Physics as I felt I was using my knowledge in a practical manner.

In my time outside of class I was involved on campus in APO (our co-ed service fraternity), the Peddler (a student run coffee cart), as a First Year Mentor (where I helped a group of 15 freshman adjust to college life), and Dickinson's Sustainable Investment Group.

Now, I am a lab manager and researcher in Dr. Jeremy Manning's lab at Dartmouth College. Here I mentor a team of undergraduates, work on free-recall experiments, code in multiple languages, and have been finding myself up in the White Mountains more weekends than not.
