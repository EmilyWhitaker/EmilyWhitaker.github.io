About Me

I'm an aspriring researcher with a background in physics and environmental science. I have a passion for fluid dynamics, sensor development, and large data sets.

I was born outside of Princeton, New Jersey and grew up in a town that boasted more cows than people. When I was little I dreamed of being an engineer due to a fascination with Legos and bridges. However, a high school physics class made me see the beauty of physics.

At Dickinson I studied applied physics working it into every aspects of my classes. Some of my favorite courses were Oceanography and Environmental Physics as I felt I was using my knowdege in a practical mannner.

In my time outside of class I was involved on campus in APO (our co-ed service fraternatiy), the Peddler (a student run coffee cart), as a First Year Mentor (where I helped a group of 15 freshman adjust to college life), and Dickinson's Sustainable Investment Group.

Now, I am a currently a lab manager and researcher in Dr. Jeremy Manning's lab at Dartmouth College. Here I mentor a team of undergraduates, work on free-recall experiments, code in multiple languages, and have been finding myself up in the White Mountains more weekends than not.

University of Wisconsin Madison Atmospheric Science and Oceanic Sciences

REU: Summer 2015

My graduate research was focused on how synchronized brain activity (i.e. neural oscillations) contributes to memory formation. To do this, I primarily used EEG/MEG to measure electrical activity as people study some information, and then related the brain activity to whether or not the person remembers it on a later test. Simply put, my work investigates how the brain forms memories and what makes them stick.

Northeastern University - Bachelor of Science

Graduated: May 2009

I graduated with a bachelor's degree in psychology from Northeastern University, concentrating my coursework and research in the field of behavioral neuroscience. What's unique about Northeastern is that classroom learning is interleaved with full-time "real-world" internships. On one such internship, I worked in a research lab that studied the impact of stress and drugs of abuse on learning and memory. It was here that my mild obsession with science and data analysis really began.
